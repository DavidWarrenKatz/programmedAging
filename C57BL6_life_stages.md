# Life Stages for C57BL/6 Mice

- **Embryogenesis**: Fertilization to E12
- **Fetal Stage**: E12–(21 or 23)
- **Infancy**: Birth to 3 weeks   
- **Childhood**: 3 weeks to 6 weeks 
- **Adolescence**: 6 weeks to 8 weeks 
- **Young Adulthood**: 2 months to 6 months 
- **Adulthood**: 7 months to 14 months
- **Youngest-old**: 15 months to 18 months
- **Old**: 19 months to 24 months
- **Oldest-old**: 25 months to 30 months
- **Centenarians**: 30–36 months
- **Supercentenarians**:  36+ months

## **Bright Lines in the Development/Aging Program for C57BL/6 Mice**

### **Table 1: Female C57BL/6 Mice**
| **Stage**                       | **Age (Days)** | **Milestone**        | **Details**                                      |
|---------------------------------|----------------|---------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| **Fetal Stage**                 | E12–E21/23     | Loss of Regenerative Ability     | Initial loss of regenerative capacity occurs as organogenesis progresses. |
| **Marks the Beginning of Adolescence** | 28-30          | Vaginal Opening                 | Also marks the first ovulation; signals puberty onset. |
| **Young Adulthood**             | 2 months       | Peak Fertility                   | Regular estrous cycles and high reproductive success. |
| **Old Age**                     | >24 months     | Loss of Reproductive Cycle       | Permanent cessation of estrous cycles.           |

---

### **Table 2: Male C57BL/6 Mice**
| **Stage**                       | **Age (Days)** | **Milestone**        | **Details**                                      |
|---------------------------------|----------------|---------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| **Fetal Stage**                 | E12–E21/23     | Loss of Regenerative Ability     | Regenerative decline as organogenesis completes. |
| **Marks the Beginning of Adolescence** | 30-35          | Preputial Separation            | Indicates onset of puberty and sperm production. |
| **Young Adulthood**             | 2 months       | Peak Sperm Production            | High reproductive capacity and fertility.        |
| **Reproductive Decline**        | >15 months     | Decreased Sperm Quality          | Gradual reduction in fertility and sperm function. |
