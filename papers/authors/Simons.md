# Mirre Simons

Dr. Mirre J. P. Simons studies the evolutionary biology and aging. His research primarily focuses on understanding the genetic and environmental factors that influence lifespan, using model organisms such as *Drosophila melanogaster* to explore how diet, genetics, and environmental stressors impact longevity. 
---

## Key Publications

### 1. [**The relationship between longevity and diet is genotype dependent and sensitive to desiccation in *Drosophila melanogaster***  ](https://doi.org/10.1242/jeb.230185) 
**McCracken, A. W., Buckle, E., & Simons, M. J. P., Journal of Experimental Biology, 2020**  

**Summary**:  
This paper explores how longevity in *Drosophila melanogaster* is influenced by diet and genotype, and how these relationships are further modulated by environmental factors such as desiccation. Simons and his team demonstrate that the effects of dietary interventions on lifespan are not universal but are instead highly dependent on genetic background. Furthermore, they show that desiccation stress significantly alters the impact of diet on longevity, indicating that environmental factors must be considered when assessing the relationship between diet and lifespan.

**Commentary**:  
This research underscores the complexity of dietary interventions in extending lifespan and highlights the importance of considering both genetic and environmental variability. The findings suggest that genotype-specific responses to diet could explain some of the contradictory results observed in longevity studies, where dietary restrictions may benefit some genetic backgrounds but not others. Simonsâ€™ work pushes forward our understanding of the intricate balance between diet, genetics, and environmental factors in determining lifespan.
